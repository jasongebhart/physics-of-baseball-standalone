Week 1: Introduction to the Physics of Motion

This week, we'll lay the foundation for our journey into the physics of baseball by exploring the fundamental concepts of motion.

Learning Objectives

  • Define and differentiate between distance, displacement, speed, velocity, and acceleration.
  • Apply the concepts of motion to describe the movement of a baseball.
  • Use video analysis to qualitatively analyze motion.

üé≠ Types of Motion in Baseball

Linear Motion

Motion in a straight line, such as a fastball traveling from pitcher to catcher

Examples: Fastball delivery, base running

Rotational Motion

Motion around an axis, such as a pitcher's arm rotation during windup

Examples: Pitcher windup, ball spin

Projectile Motion

Curved motion under gravity's influence, such as the arc of a fly ball

Examples: Fly balls, pop-ups

Combined Motion

Motion involving both linear and rotational components simultaneously

Examples: Curveball, slider
