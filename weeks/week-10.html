Integrating Physics and Pitching

Physics Principles in Pitching

Newton's Laws: Ground force → Body acceleration → Ball velocity

Work-Energy: W = ΔKE = ½mv²_f - ½mv²_i

Impulse-Momentum: F_avg × Δt = mΔv

Angular Momentum: L = Iω (conserved during rotation)

Integration: All principles work simultaneously in efficient pitching delivery

Arm Acceleration

Final energy concentration in forearm and ball release.

The Kinetic Chain in Action

Sequential Energy Transfer

Stage 1: Ground Force (Legs)
  • Generate reaction force from mound
  • Create stable base for rotation
Stage 2: Hip Rotation (Core)
  • Transfer linear to angular momentum
  • Accelerate body's center of mass
Stage 3: Trunk & Shoulder (Torso)
  • External rotation builds elastic energy
  • Prepare for explosive internal rotation
Stage 4: Arm & Release (Final)
  • Convert stored energy to ball velocity
  • Precise timing maximizes efficiency

Week 10 Summary: Key Concepts

Newton's Laws in Pitching

Ground reaction forces create action-reaction pairs. F_net = ma drives acceleration

Work-Energy Transfer

W = ΔKE. Energy flows sequentially from legs → core → arm → ball

Impulse-Momentum Application

F_avg × Δt = mΔv. Longer force application time increases ball velocity

Kinetic Chain Efficiency

Sequential activation: legs → hips → torso → shoulder → arm maximizes power
